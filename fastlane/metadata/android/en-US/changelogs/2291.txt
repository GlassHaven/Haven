v5.5.1: broaden et-kotlin R8 keep rule (good hygiene)

The proguard rule covering the et-kotlin submodule was scoped
to `sh.haven.et.protocol.**` only, leaving
`sh.haven.et.transport.EtTransport`, `sh.haven.et.crypto.EtCrypto`,
and the top-level `EtLogger` exposed to R8. The v5.5.0 release
mapping confirmed R8 was renaming EtTransport to h5.b and
stripping the ET infrastructure classes. Broadened the rule to
`sh.haven.et.**` to mirror the existing `sh.haven.mosh.**` rule
that already covers the mosh submodule.

Caveat — this is a structural correction, not a validated
causal fix for a reported runtime failure. A user reported
Eternal Terminal failing in v5.4.5 release; a post-fix bisect
on signed v5.4.5 against the same host could not reproduce the
failure. Whatever broke ET originally was something else
(likely transient network or profile state) and self-resolved.
Pure-Kotlin renames across a closed graph should be safe at
runtime — there is no reflection in the et-kotlin source. The
keep rule fix is worth keeping because the rule shape was
demonstrably wrong; it is not worth claiming as a bug fix.
