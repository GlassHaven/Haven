v5.6.1: HLS streaming fix for 1080p+ sources

libx264 -preset ultrafast silently forces a Constrained Baseline
bitstream regardless of -profile:v main. Chrome's MSE refuses
Constrained Baseline above ~720p, so every 1080p or 4K stream
(local, SFTP, rclone) failed with MEDIA_ERR_SRC_NOT_SUPPORTED —
the HLS player browser tab showed "Video error: code 4" and
nothing else. 720p sources were unaffected, which is why the
bug slipped through.

Fix: use -preset veryfast, which actually honours Main profile
and is still real-time on a Pixel-class device. Also drops the
-tune zerolatency flag (live-streaming knob, no VOD benefit,
bloats output ~2.7x at veryfast).

Verified on a Pixel 8 Pro against a real SFTP source with nine
generated test files covering H.264 720p/1080p/4K/HEVC/VP9,
portrait 55fps screen recording, 23.976 cinema fps, 10-bit
High 10, audio-only MP3, and MKV with embedded SRT subtitles —
all play on-device after the fix. ffprobe on the pre-fix
segment confirms profile=Constrained Baseline; post-fix
confirms profile=Main.

Only touches core/ffmpeg/HlsStreamServer.kt; no other changes.
