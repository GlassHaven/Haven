v5.20.0-rc1: floating IME composer (#96)

Release candidate for focused CJK testing. Not yet the stable
release — please report issues.

The terminal's IME path used to project in-flight composition text
directly into the terminal, one character at a time, then erase
and rewrite it on every candidate change. For CJK input (romaji →
hiragana → kanji, pinyin conversion, etc.) that looked like
flicker; over mosh / ET the per-keystroke round-trips made it
painful.

v5.20.0-rc1 keeps the composition in a **floating overlay** near
the terminal cursor. The partial text is visible to you but never
reaches the remote shell. When the IME commits (you tap a
candidate), the final text lands in the terminal in one go — no
flicker, no ghost backspaces.

The overlay is blue to distinguish it from the green compose-key
buffer. English / Latin input is unchanged because those IMEs
don't use composition. Swipe typing on Gboard also goes through
the overlay and commits as a single word on lift.

Please test with your primary IME and report back on #96.
