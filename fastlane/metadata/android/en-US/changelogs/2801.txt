v5.24.16: fix app crash when an ffmpeg job is cancelled or killed

FfmpegJob's stderr / stdout reader threads didn't wrap their read loop
in a try/catch, so whenever the ffmpeg subprocess exited unexpectedly —
cancel, Android killing it as a PhantomProcessRecord during a long
transcode, or the stream being closed from another thread — the
reader's blocking read() threw InterruptedIOException and that
propagated as a FATAL EXCEPTION, taking the whole app down.

Reader threads now swallow IOException quietly. The job status still
comes from process.exitValue(), so cancellations and OS kills surface
cleanly instead of crashing.

Visible symptom: HLS streaming a large 4K HEVC file (long transcode
times) could crash Haven mid-stream. The crash is fixed. Whether the
transcode itself completes on a given device still depends on
Android's phantom-process policy, which a follow-up foreground
service will address.
