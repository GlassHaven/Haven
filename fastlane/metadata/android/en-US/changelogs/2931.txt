v5.24.29: drag-with-button in touchpad mode (#107)

Touchpad mode now supports drag-with-button-1 — moving windows,
selecting text, dragging scrollbars — via the standard trackpad
"tap-then-drag" gesture (RealVNC convention).

How it works:

- Single tap → click (immediate, as before).
- Tap, then within 300 ms touch down again and move → drag with
  button 1 held. Cursor follows finger; button 1 releases on lift.
- Tap, then within 300 ms tap again without moving → second click
  (effectively a double-click via two onTap callbacks).
- Plain long-press (no preceding tap) → right-click at cursor
  (unchanged).

The 300 ms follow-up window also suppresses the long-press
right-click timer for that touch — so tap-tap-hold-still doesn't
misfire as a phantom right-click. Plain hold (no preceding tap)
still triggers right-click.

Implemented for both VNC and RDP. Direct touch mode is
unchanged in both protocols.
