v5.24.30: low-bandwidth auto-suggest (#107 final)

Last item from #107 — VNC sessions on slow connections now surface
a non-blocking banner offering a one-tap reconnect at 256 colours.

How it works:
- A CountingInputStream wraps the (post-handshake, post-TLS) VNC
  input stream. ThroughputTracker keeps a 10-second rolling window
  of (timestamp, bytesRead) samples.
- The client event loop samples once per iteration. After 10s of
  data and if measured throughput is sustained below 1 Mbps AND the
  current colour depth is 24-bit, fires a one-shot suggestion via
  VncConfig.onBandwidthSuggestion.
- DesktopViewModel surfaces the suggestion as a StateFlow on the
  Vnc tab. The viewer renders a non-blocking banner above the
  framebuffer: "Slow connection — switch to 256 colours? [Switch]
  [Dismiss]".
- Switch persists vncColorDepth on the profile (if any), closes the
  current tab, and reconnects with the new depth.
- Dismiss clears the banner; suggestion is one-shot per session so
  it won't keep nagging.

Why suggest-don't-auto: mid-session SetPixelFormat is fiddly with
ZRLE/ZLIB because the zlib streams are session-continuous (RFC
6143) — there's no clean framing marker between in-flight 4-byte
pixels and new 1-byte pixels. A reconnect with the new depth is
safe and idiomatic.

New global preference (Settings → "Suggest 256 colours on slow VNC
links", default on) silences the banner entirely for users who
want to manage colour depth themselves.

This closes the open follow-ups from #107. Touchpad mode, drag-
with-button, RDP touchpad parity, and bandwidth-suggest are all in.
