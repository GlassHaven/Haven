v5.24.66: black-bg residue after exiting nano / vim with the keyboard up (#120)

@agevlakh reported on #120 that exiting `nano` over an SSH session
left a band of solid-black-background lines in the scrollback. The
text content of those lines is correct (post-exit shell prompts) but
the cell background is pure indexed-0 black instead of the theme
default; subsequent output renders normally, so the corruption is
confined to a few specific rows.

Pixel-sampling the reporter's video confirms the cells really do
have a non-default bg baked in (RGB 0,0,0 vs the theme's RGB 35,37,30),
and the maintainer reproduced it locally — a single tab-switch resize
clears the artefact, but rapid resizes during the IME inset animation
trigger it.

Cause: `Terminal.kt`'s resize `LaunchedEffect` was keyed on
`availableWidth/Height` and called `terminalEmulator.resize(...)` with
no debounce. While the IME insets animate, Compose's `onSizeChanged`
fires many times per second, so each frame propagates straight through
to `vterm_set_size`. libvterm's `resize_buffer` fills newly-cleared
cells with `screen->pen` for both the active and inactive buffers —
so while a foreground app (nano, vim) is mid-redraw with its menu-bar
pen still active, those colours get baked into rows of the *primary*
buffer too. After the app exits the alt-screen, those rows scroll into
scrollback as the user keeps typing.

Fix: debounce the resize coroutine with a short (80 ms) delay. Each
new size cancels the in-flight `LaunchedEffect`, so only the final
stable size completes the delay and reaches the emulator — the same
"single resize" path that already worked when switching tabs. The
first resize after the emulator is constructed bypasses the delay so
initial paint stays snappy.

No libvterm change yet; the inactive-buffer-resize-with-active-pen is
a real bug in upstream too, but the debounce is enough to ship the
user-visible fix.
