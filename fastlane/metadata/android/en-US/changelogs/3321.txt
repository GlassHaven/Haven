v5.24.68: disconnect-countdown banner for stalled Mosh sessions

When a Mosh session goes silent — typing `exit` inside the shell, the
remote host vanishing, the network falling away — the client used to
sit unresponsive for up to 8 seconds before the SESSION_DEAD
heuristic fired and tore the transport down. There was no signal in
the UI to say "the connection's gone, give me a moment" — just a
frozen terminal.

Now `MoshTransport` exposes a `secondsUntilDisconnect: StateFlow<Int?>`
that flips non-null once we're past the keepalive window with no
inbound packets, and counts down from ~4s to the SESSION_DEAD
deadline. The terminal screen shows a small top-of-pane banner
("Disconnected — closing in N s") while it's non-null, switching to
"Disconnected — closing" at zero. Any received packet (recovery,
reconnect) clears the banner instantly.

Mosh-only for now. ET is TCP and signals close more cleanly so
doesn't need it; SSH disconnect already has its own reconnect-state
indicator on the tab.

The underlying SESSION_DEAD timeout itself is unchanged — the
visibility change is the whole win.
