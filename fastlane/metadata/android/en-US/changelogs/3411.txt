v5.24.77: FIDO2 SSH on Android 14+ — fix the PendingIntent crash (#15)

olmari (#15) hit an immediate crash on a Pixel 9 Fold (Android 15) the
moment they tried to use an imported ED25519 SK key with their YubiKey:

    sh.haven.app: Targeting U+ (version 34 and above) disallows
    creating or retrieving a PendingIntent with FLAG_MUTABLE, an
    implicit Intent ... use FLAG_IMMUTABLE.

Cause: FidoAuthenticator's USB-permission request built a
PendingIntent around an implicit Intent (`Intent(ACTION_USB_PERMISSION)`
with no package set) and Haven's targetSdk is 35. Android 14+ blocks
that combination — UsbManager.requestPermission fills the
EXTRA_PERMISSION_GRANTED flag back into the intent so we must keep
FLAG_MUTABLE, which means the Intent itself has to be made explicit.

Fix: set `.setPackage(context.packageName)` on the permission Intent
before wrapping it in the PendingIntent. The broadcast still only
reaches Haven's own receiver (it always did — the IntentFilter is on
our action), but Android 14+'s safety check now sees an explicit
target and lets the MUTABLE flag through.

Untested with a physical YubiKey on Android 14+ in this session;
relies on olmari (or any FIDO2-key user) to confirm. The change is
one line + an explanatory comment.
