v5.24.90: nano-exit garbage attempt fix (#120)

agevlakh's report on #120: editing an existing file in nano then
exiting leaves stale content in scrollback and the cursor at the
top of the screen. Ctrl+L clears it. Other full-screen tools
(vim, less, htop) don't repro. The original .66 fix targeted a
different trigger and doesn't cover this case.

Working theory: the render-cache (currentLines + pending damage
regions) drifts out of sync with libvterm's primary buffer during
the alt-screen window. On exit, the renderer paints whatever
stale state the cache held; Ctrl+L heals it because the shell
writes new content over the stale cells.

Termlib hook on VTERM_PROP_ALTSCREEN: when alt-screen transitions
OFF, queue a full-screen redamage and let processPendingUpdates
re-pull every cell from libvterm — same effect Ctrl+L produces,
but automatic. Activation path left alone; alt-screen entry
always paints fresh.

Untested in the wild yet — needs agevlakh to retest the same
edit-existing-file repro. If the libvterm state itself is
corrupted (not just our cache), this won't help and we'll have
to look at the primary-buffer save/restore pair around resize.
